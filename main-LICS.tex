%% article.
\begin{abstract}
    An infinite structure has, given a field, the finite length property if the following holds for each of its finite powers:
    in the corresponding free vector space, strict chains of equivariant subspaces are bounded in length.
    It has been  shown that the countable pure set and the dense linear ordering without endpoints have this property.
    In this paper, we generalise these two results to
    a)~interpretations in smoothly approximable structures, provided the field has characteristic zero, and
    b)~generaically ordered expansions of Fraïssé limits with free amalgamation over a graph language.
    As a special case, we prove the finite length property of the Rado graph using both methods.
\end{abstract}
